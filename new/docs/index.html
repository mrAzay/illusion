Einstein Hollow Face Illusion
The Hollow Face Illusion is probably one of the most amazing optical illusions we have come across. How does it work? Richard Dawkins mentions this illusion in his recent book, "The God Delusion". When we see something, our view of the world has been processed by our brain. And our brain 'knows' that a face is convex, not concave, so that is the way we see it. When we move, the face does not move in the way that a convex face would move, so our brain comes up with a different explanation, and we see the face move, following us wherever we go!
Penrose Triangle
The Penrose triangle, also known as the Penrose tribar, or the impossible tribar, is a triangular object, an optical illusion consisting of an object which can be depicted in a perspective drawing, but cannot exist as a solid object. For a long time it was considered impossible to build a Penrose triangle in real space. We created it! This figure looks like a triangle with only one single shell. But not everything is as it seems.
Reverspective picture
It looks like an ordinary picture, when viewed from the point from which we usually view the works of masters in museums. However, when you change the viewing angle, the contents of the picture begin to change as if you yourself are part of a 3D work, where you can see all the subtleties of work from different angles. When the principles of perspective get mixed up and harden in the picture, something extraordinary will happen: the mind will be deceived to the extent possible and the static picture can move by itself.
Hidden Message Perspective Illusion
Wise people say, what you see depends on your point of view. This principle underlies our interaction with the world. Clouds in the sky can be a set of random blots, and can paint a beautiful picture. From a bird's eye view, the buildings of the metropolis look like a holistic picture. The correct point of view makes our lives more meaningful. All you need is to follow your vision. Installation "Follow a vision" will help you find your own vision and use it to the maximum.
Levitating objects
It is believed that it is impossible to violate the laws of physics. Ancient legends tell about great magicians who could make various objects float in the air, calling this kind of magic levitation. For a long time, levitation was considered quackery. Today, we have realized levitation in reality, using modern technology and a little ingenuity. The object floats in the air, showing us the miracle of the magnetic field. Thus, the inviolability of the laws of physics stems from the limitations of our knowledge.
Interactive 3D Zoetrope
A zoetrope is one of several pre-film animation devices that produce the illusion of motion by displaying a sequence of drawings or photographs showing progressive phases of that motion. Victorian animators used spinning displays called zoetropes to give the illusion of images in action. 150 years have passed, but the zoetropes have retained a kind of cult of worship and respect among animated circles. 3D Zoetrope takes it to the next level, animating solid, 3D objects instead of paper cartoons. They are real-time stop-motion animation machines.
Infinity light tunnel
It is assumed that any tunnel has a beginning and an end. This illusion shows an endless tunnel - a colorful abyss that begins to play with those who peer into it. Bright lights will allow you to look into the abyss as far as possible. Expand your imagination and treat yourself to a dizzying light show.
